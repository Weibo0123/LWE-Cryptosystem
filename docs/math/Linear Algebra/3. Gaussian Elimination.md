# **Part I — Gaussian Elimination**

### **Definition**

Gaussian Elimination:

Use valid row operations to transform a linear system into **upper-triangular form**, then solve by **back substitution**.

### **Essence**

Transform:

- complicated system  

into

- simple “solve from bottom to top” system

---

## **Geometric Intuition (2D Case)**

For two equations in two variables, each equation represents a line.

Possible configurations:

- intersect → one solution  
- parallel → no solution  
- overlap → infinitely many solutions  

### **Role of Elimination**

Elimination combines equations to remove one variable.

Example:

$$
\begin{cases}
x + y = 3 \\
2x + y = 4
\end{cases}
$$

Subtract row 1 from row 2:

$$
x = 1
$$

One variable disappears.

This is the fundamental mechanism of elimination.

---

## **Algebraic Setup (Matrix Form)**

A linear system is written as:

$$
Ax = b
$$

Augmented matrix form:

$$
[A \mid b]
$$

Example:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 1 & \mid & 3 \\
2 & 1 & \mid & 4
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

## **Allowed Row Operations**

These operations **do not change the solution set**.

### **(1) Row swap**

$$
R_i \leftrightarrow R_j
$$

### **(2) Multiply by nonzero scalar**

$$
R_i \leftarrow c R_i \quad (c \ne 0)
$$

### **(3) Row replacement**

$$
R_i \leftarrow R_i + c R_j
$$

These operations create **equivalent systems**.

---

## **Gaussian Elimination Algorithm**

### **Step 1 — Forward Elimination**

Goal:

- make all entries below the diagonal equal to $0$

Target shape:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}* & * & * \\
0 & * & * \\
0 & 0 & *
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Procedure:

- choose pivot  
- eliminate entries below  
- proceed column by column  

---

### **Step 2 — Back Substitution**

Solve from bottom upward:

$$
z = \dots
$$

$$
y = \dots
$$

$$
x = \dots
$$

---

## **Standard Example**

Solve:

$$
\begin{cases}
x + y = 3 \\
2x + y = 4
\end{cases}
$$

### **Matrix**

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 1 & \mid & 3 \\
2 & 1 & \mid & 4
\end{bmatrix}
$$

### **Eliminate**

$$
R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1
$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 1 & \mid & 3 \\
0 & -1 & \mid & -2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

### **Back substitute**

$$
y = 2
$$

$$
x = 1
$$

---

## **Conceptual Understanding**

Core principle:

Gaussian elimination uses **linear combinations of rows** to remove variables.

Thus:

- elimination = linear combination process

# **Part II — Modular Gaussian Elimination**

Used in cryptography and LWE.

---

## **Key Difference from Standard Arithmetic**

### **Standard**

- division available  

### **Modular**

- division not defined  
- multiply by inverse instead  

Replace:

$$
\frac{1}{a}
\quad \longrightarrow \quad
a^{-1} \pmod{q}
$$

---

## **Modular Intuition**

- numbers wrap around (clock arithmetic)  
- every operation reduced modulo $q$

All computations are performed in:

$$
\mathbb{Z}_q
$$

---

## **Rule Modification**

### **Standard**

$$
R_i \leftarrow \frac{1}{a} R_i
$$

### **Modular**

$$
R_i \leftarrow a^{-1} R_i \pmod{q}
$$

---

## **Modular Gaussian Elimination Algorithm**

Working in $\mathbb{Z}_q$:

### **Step 1**

After every operation:

- reduce modulo $q$

### **Step 2**

If pivot is $a$, compute:

$$
a^{-1} \pmod{q}
$$

using:

- Extended Euclidean algorithm  
- or `pow(a, -1, q)` in Python  

### **Step 3**

Normalize pivot row:

$$
R_i \leftarrow a^{-1} R_i
$$

### **Step 4**

Eliminate as usual.

---

## **Modular Example**

Solve:

$$
\begin{cases}
2x + y \equiv 1 \pmod{5} \\
x + y \equiv 3 \pmod{5}
\end{cases}
$$

### **Matrix**

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
2 & 1 & \mid & 1 \\
1 & 1 & \mid & 3
\end{bmatrix}
$$

### **Pivot = 2**

Inverse:

$$
2^{-1} \equiv 3 \pmod{5}
$$

since:

$$
2 \cdot 3 \equiv 1
$$

### **Normalize**

$$
R_1 \leftarrow 3R_1
$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 3 & \mid & 3 \\
1 & 1 & \mid & 3
\end{bmatrix}
$$

### **Eliminate**

$$
R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - R_1
$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 3 & \mid & 3 \\
0 & 3 & \mid & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

### **Back substitute**

$$
3y \equiv 0 \Rightarrow y = 0
$$

$$
x = 3
$$

---

## **Standard vs Modular Comparison**

| | Standard | Modular |
|---|---|---|
| Number system | $\mathbb{R}$ | $\mathbb{Z}_q$ |
| Division | ordinary division | multiply inverse |
| Arithmetic | real numbers | modulo $q$ |
| Applications | linear algebra | cryptography / LWE |

---

## **Summary**

### **Standard Gaussian elimination**

- triangularize  
- back substitute  
- eliminate using row combinations  

### **Modular version**

- all operations mod $q$  
- replace division with inverse multiplication
